  • Go to lawyers, draft documents
  • Find investors or list on crowdfunding site
  • Vet investors, do KYC and AML checks, lots of phone calls
  • Investors sign documents and send them back

  • Tell investors to get a Pillar wallet
  • Investors do their own KYC on the wallet (and they can reuse that KYC for many other things as well)
  • Investors wire money to their Pillar wallet
  • Investors buy as much as they want
  • Investors see equity tokens in their wallets immediately, blockchain holds all tokens, cap table is always up-to-date
  • After the lock-up period, equity tokens are tradable on our exchange

An equity token is a cryptographic token

An equity token is a cryptographic token that gives its owner the same rights as if owning shares in a corporation. The rights are both ownership and control. In the world of paper (or digital) shares, the company maintains a registry of who owns how many shares, and the company manages things like elections, votes, and transfer of control according to written by-laws.

The goal of equity tokens is that people will own and manage their own tokens, trade them, use them for voting and control issues, the blockchain acts as the cap table, and a set of smart contracts can codify the by-laws. We are years away from this scenario, so in the meantime we are providing the ownership part and working on the control systems. This will involve software, standards, and in most countries amendments to the security laws.

Token Factory has worked with law firms like Eversheds Southerland, Latham Watkins, Allen Ovary, and others to ensure that ownership of a token legally represents the ownership of a part of each company. We are also working on an international, open-source equity token standard to make equity tokens interoperable between exchanges.

For now, equity tokens will be available to accredited/qualified investors in the countries where we operate.
