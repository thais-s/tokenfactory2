An equity token is a cryptographic token

and We are the Equity Token Leaders

An equity token is a cryptographic token that gives its owner the same rights as if owning shares in a corporation. The rights are both ownership and control. In the world of paper (or digital) shares, the company maintains a registry of who owns how many shares, and the company manages things like elections, votes, and transfer of control according to written by-laws.

The goal of equity tokens is that people will own and manage their own tokens, trade them, use them for voting and control issues, the blockchain acts as the cap table, and a set of smart contracts can codify the by-laws. We are years away from this scenario, so in the meantime we are providing the ownership part and working on the control systems. This will involve software, standards, and in most countries amendments to the security laws.

Token Factory has worked with law firms like Eversheds Southerland, Latham Watkins, Allen Ovary, and others to ensure that ownership of a token legally represents the ownership of a part of each company. We are also working on an international, open-source equity token standard to make equity tokens interoperable between exchanges.

For now, equity tokens will be available to accredited/qualified investors in the countries where we operate.

ANSWER & QUESTION

Will you conduct our stock offering?

Legally, we can provide companies with the tools to issue their own equity via tokens. As our systems get richer, we’ll be able to provide more tools for you to conduct votes, surveys, send out reports, provide data feeds, and much more.

Will you tokenize all the terms in our term sheet?

No. We will give companies a very standard menu with a few options, which reduces expense and makes tokens tradable later.

How do you onboard companies?

Simply fill out our onboarding wizard, answer the questions, and we’ll get back to you to set up a conversation.

Do you provide all the legal documentation?

Yes. We provide you with an operating agreement, shareholder agreement, by-laws, and subscription documents. Everything will be on the web site, where investors can see your pitch deck, due diligence documents, offering documents, and communicate with you.

How do investors buy our tokens?

They will either wire you money and you’ll give them tokens in their Pillar wallet, or they will purchase fiat tokens (pounds, euros) and make the trade right in the Pillar wallet.

Can Investors Trade the Tokens Right Away?

No. There will be a lock-up period of 12-24 months, depending on what we set up. Instant liquidity for new companies is a bad idea.

How much does it cost?

We’ll give you a quote. An offering of a UK company to UK investors is generally around £50k.

What Happens After the Offering?

You’ll need to commit to providing written shareholder reports and financial data quarterly. You can conduct further offerings as needed.

Who can Buy our Shares?

Only qualified investors in the UK plus up to 150 members of the public, according to UK securities laws.

Do you also provide marketing services?

Not at this time, but we plan to in the future.
